**23rd USENIX Security Symposium**
**August 20–22, 2014 • San Diego, CA**

The USENIX Security Symposium is a premier event that brings together researchers, practitioners, system administrators, system programmers, and other professionals interested in the latest advancements in computer systems and network security. The symposium will feature a three-day technical program, including refereed papers, invited talks, poster sessions, panel discussions, and Birds-of-a-Feather (BoF) sessions.

**Featured Speakers and Sessions:**

- **Keynote Address:**
  - *Speaker:* Phil Lapsley, author of "Exploding the Phone: The Untold Story of the Teenagers and Outlaws Who Hacked Ma Bell"

- **Panel Discussion:**
  - *Topic:* "The Future of Crypto: Getting from Here to Guarantees"
  - *Panelists:* Daniel J. Bernstein, Matt Blaze, and Tanja Lange

- **Invited Talks:**
  - *Title:* "Battling Human Trafficking with Big Data"
    - *Speaker:* Rolando R. Lopez, Orphan Secure
  - *Title:* "Insight into the NSA’s Weakening of Crypto Standards"
    - *Speaker:* Joseph Menn, Reuters
