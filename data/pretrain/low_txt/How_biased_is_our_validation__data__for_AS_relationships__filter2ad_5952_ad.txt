### Research Space and Future Directions

Given the findings from ¬ß4.2, we advocate for a more comprehensive and nuanced approach to future research efforts in this domain. Specifically, we recommend that:

1. **Diverse Goals**: Future research should be evaluated against a broader and more diverse set of goals to ensure a more holistic understanding of the subject matter.
2. **Handling Spurious Labels**: There should be more careful and explicit handling of spurious labels, sibling relationships, and complex relationships during validation efforts. This will help in improving the robustness and reliability of the results.

#### Performance Metrics and Validation Coverage

Some of the link classes for which the inference algorithms perform poorly have higher validation coverage. In this section, we show that there is no significant correlation between these two metrics. We conducted the following experiment:

- **Experiment Setup**: We uniformly sampled a subset of relationships and tracked their evaluation performance using the metrics discussed in ¬ß6. ### Potential Features for Identifying Hard Links

The following per-link metrics might help identify additional groups of 'hard links':

1. Visibility over time
2. Number of prefixes redistributed via the link
3. Number of addresses covered by those prefixes
4. Number of prefixes originated through the link
5. Number of addresses covered by those prefixes
6. Number of ASes that can observe (i.e., occur left from) the link
7. Number of ASes that might receive traffic via (i.e., occur right from) the link
