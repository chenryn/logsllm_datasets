#### Bit-Squatting
Bit-squatting is a technique where an attacker registers a domain name that is one bit different from a legitimate domain, hoping to catch typos or errors. This can be used to redirect traffic or perform other malicious activities.

#### Misunderstood End-Point Behavior
Misunderstood end-point behavior can lead to security vulnerabilities. Understanding how DNS resolution works, including suffix search paths and DNS devolution, is crucial.

##### Expected Resolver Behavior
- **DNS Suffix Search Paths**: The resolver appends domain suffixes to the query to find the correct domain.
- **Poorly Documented Behavior**: Some behaviors may not be well-documented, leading to unexpected results.

##### Observations and Lessons Learned
- **Suffix Search Paths**:
  - **Example**: For a domain `ad.foo.com`, the resolver might try `www.ad.foo.com` and then `www.foo.com`.
- **DNS Devolution**:
  - **XP Behavior**:
    - **Connection Specific Domain**: `phx.ad.foo.com`
    - **DNS Queries**:
      - `www.phx.ad.foo.com`
      - `www.ad.foo.com`
      - `www.foo.com`
      - `www.com`

Understanding these behaviors can help in securing your network and preventing potential attacks.
