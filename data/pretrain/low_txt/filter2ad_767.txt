## Abstract
The 17th Workshop on Privacy in the Electronic Society (WPES 2018) was held on October 15, 2018, in conjunction with the 25th ACM Conference on Computer and Communications Security (CCS 2018) in Toronto, Canada. The workshop aims to bring together privacy researchers and practitioners to discuss privacy challenges in an interconnected society and explore potential solutions. The program featured 11 full papers and 8 short papers, selected from a total of 52 submissions. Topics covered include communication privacy, data anonymization, privacy engineering, secure computation, and Web privacy.

## 1. Introduction
The Information Revolution has profoundly transformed society, leading to a significant increase in the collection, sharing, and analysis of personal data. This workshop aims to address the resulting privacy issues and their solutions. WPES 2018 is the 17th annual event, held in conjunction with the ACM CCS conference.

We invite submissions from academia, government, and industry, presenting novel research on all theoretical and practical aspects of electronic privacy, as well as experimental studies of fielded systems. We also welcome contributions from other communities, such as law and business, that provide perspectives on technological issues. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

- Attacks on data privacy

## 2. Program

Specific topics covered include Web privacy, secure computation, communication privacy, data deanonymization, and privacy engineering.
