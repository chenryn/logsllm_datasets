# Keynote Talk

**Speaker:** Dr. Edward W. Felten  
**Affiliation:** Princeton University

Dr. Edward W. Felten is a prominent figure in the fields of technology, law, and policy. His widely-read blog, *Freedom to Tinker*, offers insightful commentary on these subjects. He served as the lead computer science expert witness for the Department of Justice in the Microsoft antitrust case and has testified in several other significant legal proceedings.

Dr. Felten has also provided testimony before the Senate Commerce Committee on digital technology and regulation, as well as before the House Administration Committee on electronic voting. In 2004, he was named one of fifty worldwide science and technology leaders by *Scientific American* magazine.

## Biography

Edward W. Felten currently holds the position of Deputy U.S. Chief Technology Officer and is a Professor of Computer Science and Public Affairs at Princeton University. His research focuses on computer security and privacy, particularly in the context of media and consumer products, as well as technology law and policy. He has published approximately eighty papers in academic journals and has authored two books. His work on web security, copyright and copy protection, and electronic voting has received extensive coverage in the popular press.
