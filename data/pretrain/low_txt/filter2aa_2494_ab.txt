#### Misunderstood End-Point Behavior
Misunderstood end-point behavior can lead to unexpected DNS resolution and network issues. Key points to consider include:
- **Expected Resolver Behavior**: Understanding how DNS resolvers handle queries.
- **DNS Suffix Search Paths**: How suffixes are appended to incomplete domain names.
- **Poorly Documented Behavior**: Lack of clear documentation on how certain systems handle DNS queries.

##### Observations and Lessons Learned
- **Suffix Search Paths**: Different operating systems and configurations may append different suffixes to domain names.
- **DNS Devolution**: The process by which a resolver progressively removes subdomains from a query until it finds a match.

##### Example: XP Behavior
- **Connection Specific Domain – `phx.ad.foo.com`**
  - DNS Query: `www.phx.ad.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.ad.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.com`

##### Example: Post-XP Behavior
- **Connection Specific Domain – `phx.ad.foo.com`**
  - DNS Query: `www.phx`
  - NetBIOS Query: `www.phx`

##### Example: DNS Devolution
- **Connection Specific Domain – `phx.ad.foo.com`**
  - DNS Query: `www.phx.ad.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.ad.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.foo.com`
  - DNS Query: `www.com`

- **Connection Specific Domain – `ad.foo.co.uk`**
  - DNS Query: `www.ad.foo.co.uk`

Understanding these behaviors is crucial for diagnosing and mitigating network issues related to DNS resolution.
