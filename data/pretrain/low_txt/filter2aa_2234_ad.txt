### Mischief and Related Offenses

#### Section 430: Mischief
**Offense and Punishment:**
- If an omission is likely to cause actual danger to life, or if it constitutes mischief in relation to property or data:
  - (a) The person is guilty of an indictable offense and is liable to imprisonment for a term not exceeding five years.
  - (b) The person is guilty of an offense punishable on summary conviction.

**Exceptions:**
- No person commits mischief within the meaning of this section by reason only that:
  - (a) They stop work due to a disagreement with their employer regarding any matter relating to their employment.
  - (b) They stop work due to a disagreement between their employer and a bargaining agent acting on their behalf.
  - (c) They stop work as part of a combination of workers or employees for their own reasonable protection.

- No person commits mischief within the meaning of this section by reason only that they attend at or near, or approach a dwelling-house or place, solely for the purpose of obtaining or communicating information.

**Definition:**
- "Data" has the same meaning as in section 342.1.

**References:**
- R.S., 1985, c. C-46, s. 430; R.S., 1985, c. 27 (1st Supp.), s. 57; 1994, c. 44, s. 28.

### Germany: Data and Computer Offenses

#### Section 202a: Data Espionage
- **(1)** A person who unlawfully obtains data for themselves or another, which were not intended for them and were especially protected against unauthorized access, and who circumvents the protection, is liable to imprisonment not exceeding three years or a fine.
- **(2)** For the purposes of subsection (1), "data" refers to information stored or transmitted electronically, magnetically, or in any other manner not immediately perceivable.

#### Section 202b: Phishing
- A person who unlawfully intercepts data (as defined in section 202a(2)) not intended for them, for themselves or another, by technical means from a non-public data processing facility or from the electromagnetic broadcast of a data processing facility, is liable to imprisonment not exceeding two years or a fine, unless the offense incurs a more severe penalty under other provisions.

#### Section 202c: Acts Preparatory to Data Espionage and Phishing
- **(1)** A person who prepares the commission of an offense under section 202a or section 202b by:
  - (a) Producing, acquiring, selling, supplying, disseminating, or making otherwise accessible passwords or other security codes enabling access to data (section 202a(2)).
  - (b) Creating, acquiring, selling, supplying, disseminating, or making otherwise accessible software for the purpose of committing such an offense.
- **(2)** Section 149(2) and (3) apply mutatis mutandis.

#### Section 263a: Computer Fraud
- **(1)** A person who, with the intent of obtaining for themselves or a third person an unlawful material benefit, damages the property of another by influencing the result of a data processing operation through incorrect configuration of a program, use of incorrect or incomplete data, unauthorized use of data, or other unauthorized influence on the course of the processing, is liable to imprisonment not exceeding five years or a fine.
- **(2)** Section 263(2) to (7) apply mutatis mutandis.
- **(3)** A person who prepares an offense under subsection (1) by writing computer programs designed to commit such an act, or procures them for themselves or another, offers them for sale, or holds or supplies them to another, is liable to imprisonment not exceeding three years or a fine.
- **(4)** In cases under subsection (3), section 149(2) and (3) apply mutatis mutandis.

#### Section 303a: Data Tampering
- **(1)** A person who unlawfully deletes, suppresses, renders unusable, or alters data (as defined in section 202a(2)) is liable to imprisonment not exceeding two years or a fine.
- **(2)** An attempt to commit this offense is also punishable.

#### Section 303b: Computer Sabotage
- **(1)** A person who interferes with data processing operations of substantial importance to another by:
  - (a) Committing an offense under section 303a(1).
  - (b) Entering or transmitting data (as defined in section 202a(2)) with the intention of causing damage to another.
  - (c) Destroying, damaging, rendering unusable, removing, or altering a data processing system or a data carrier.
- **(2)** If the data processing operation is of substantial importance for another’s business, enterprise, or a public authority, the penalty is imprisonment not exceeding five years or a fine.
- **(3)** An attempt to commit this offense is also punishable.
- **(4)** In especially serious cases under subsection (2), the penalty is imprisonment from six months to ten years. An especially serious case typically occurs if the offender:
  - (a) Causes major financial loss.
  - (b) Acts on a commercial basis or as a member of a gang whose purpose is the continued commission of computer sabotage.
  - (c) Jeopardizes the population’s supply with vital goods or services or the national security of the Federal Republic of Germany.
- **(5)** Section 202c applies mutatis mutandis to acts preparatory to an offense under subsection (1).

#### Section 303c: Request to Prosecute
- In cases under sections 303 to 303b, the offense may only be prosecuted upon request, unless the prosecuting authority considers proprio motu that prosecution is required because of special public interest.

### England & Wales: Computer Misuse Act 1990

#### Section 1: Unauthorized Access to Computer Material
- **(1)** A person is guilty of an offense if:
  - (a) They cause a computer to perform any function with the intent to secure access to any program or data held in any computer, or to enable such access to be secured.
  - (b) The access they intend to secure, or to enable to be secured, is unauthorized.
  - (c) They know at the time when they cause the computer to perform the function that the access is unauthorized.
- **(2)** The intent need not be directed at any particular program or data, a program or data of any particular kind, or a program or data held in any particular computer.
- **(3)** A person guilty of an offense under this section is liable:
  - (a) On summary conviction in England and Wales, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (b) On summary conviction in Scotland, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (c) On conviction on indictment, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding two years or to a fine or both.

#### Section 3: Unauthorized Acts with Intent to Impair Operation of Computer, etc.
- **(1)** A person is guilty of an offense if:
  - (a) They do any unauthorized act in relation to a computer.
  - (b) At the time when they do the act, they know that it is unauthorized.
  - (c) Either subsection (2) or (3) applies.
- **(2)** This subsection applies if the person intends by doing the act:
  - (a) To impair the operation of any computer.
  - (b) To prevent or hinder access to any program or data held in any computer.
  - (c) To impair the operation of any such program or the reliability of any such data.
  - (d) To enable any of the things mentioned in paragraphs (a) to (c) to be done.
- **(3)** This subsection applies if the person is reckless as to whether the act will do any of the things mentioned in paragraphs (a) to (d) of subsection (2).
- **(4)** The intention referred to in subsection (2) or the recklessness referred to in subsection (3) need not relate to any particular computer, program, or data, or a program or data of any particular kind.
- **(5)** In this section:
  - (a) A reference to doing an act includes a reference to causing an act to be done.
  - (b) "Act" includes a series of acts.
  - (c) A reference to impairing, preventing, or hindering something includes a reference to doing so temporarily.
- **(6)** A person guilty of an offense under this section is liable:
  - (a) On summary conviction in England and Wales, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (b) On summary conviction in Scotland, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (c) On conviction on indictment, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding ten years or to a fine or both.

#### Section 3A: Making, Supplying, or Obtaining Articles for Use in Computer Misuse Offenses
- **(1)** A person is guilty of an offense if they make, adapt, supply, or offer to supply any article intending it to be used to commit, or to assist in the commission of, an offense under section 1 or 3.
- **(2)** A person is guilty of an offense if they supply or offer to supply any article believing that it is likely to be used to commit, or to assist in the commission of, an offense under section 1 or 3.
- **(3)** A person is guilty of an offense if they obtain any article with a view to its being supplied for use to commit, or to assist in the commission of, an offense under section 1 or 3.
- **(4)** In this section, "article" includes any program or data held in electronic form.
- **(5)** A person guilty of an offense under this section is liable:
  - (a) On summary conviction in England and Wales, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (b) On summary conviction in Scotland, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months or to a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum or both.
  - (c) On conviction on indictment, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding two years or to a fine or both.
