## Finding Your Creative Space
- **Identify Environments:** Determine where you are most creative, whether it be in a quiet library, a bustling café, or a serene park.
- **Avoid Boredom:** Engage in interesting meetings, classes, and talks to stimulate your mind.

## Research Campaigns
- **Long-Term Goals:** Focus on long-term research objectives.
- **Inform Decision Makers:** Keep stakeholders informed about your progress and findings.
- **Communicate with Different Audiences:** Tailor your communication to different groups, such as policymakers, academics, and the general public.
- **Develop a Research Vision:** Clearly define your research goals and how they contribute to the field.

## Research Funding
- **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR):** Explore these programs for funding opportunities.
- **National Science Foundation (NSF) and Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA):** Consider these agencies for additional funding.
- **Challenges:** Be prepared for competition, extensive documentation, and potential strings attached to the funding.

## Scientific Method
1. **Ask a Question**
2. **Do Background Research**
3. **Construct a Hypothesis**
4. **Test Your Hypothesis by Doing an Experiment**
5. **Analyze Your Data and Draw a Conclusion**
6. **Report Your Results (Was Your Hypothesis Correct?)**

## Rigor and Merit (NSF Review Criteria)
- **Intellectual Merit:**
  - Importance to advancing knowledge and understanding.
  - Qualifications of the proposer.
  - Creativity, originality, and transformative concepts.
  - Project organization and resource access.
- **Broader Impacts:**
  - Advancing discovery and understanding.
  - Promoting teaching, training, and learning.
  - Inclusion of underrepresented groups.
  - Broad dissemination of results.
  - Benefits to society.

## Institutional Review Board (IRB)
- **Role:** Approves, monitors, and reviews research involving human subjects.
- **History:** Established in response to research abuses, including the Tuskegee Syphilis Study.
- **Requirements:** If your research involves human subjects, you may need IRB approval.

## Responsible Disclosure
- **Debate:** Balancing social responsibility and false security.
- **Timing:** Determining the appropriate time to disclose a vulnerability.
- **Ethics:** Consider the ethical implications of your actions.

## Keeping Personal Research Distinct from Work
- **Use Your Own Time and Resources:** Ensure that your personal work does not overlap with your employer's resources.
- **Read Contracts Carefully:** Understand your employment contract and any non-disclosure agreements (NDAs).
- **Respect Intellectual Property (IP):** Smart employers and schools will respect your personal IP.

## Miscellaneous Tips
- **No Dual Submissions:** Avoid submitting the same work to multiple venues.
- **Conference Travel and Honorariums:** Most academic conferences do not cover travel expenses or honorariums for speakers.
- **Seek Feedback Thoughtfully:** Ask specific, thoughtful questions rather than asking someone to read your entire paper.
- **Author Contributions:** Authors are typically listed from the first (biggest contribution) to the last (least contribution).
- **Acknowledge and Cite:** When in doubt, acknowledge or cite others' work.
- **Discretion:** Be discreet, especially when dealing with sensitive or proprietary research.

## Academia and Industry
- **Follow the Money:** Research grants, fads, and customers with money drive both academia and industry.
- **Industry Requirements:** Must make a case for the bottom line.
- **Academic Advantages:** Passion and intellectual freedom.
- **Academic Rank:** Instructor, Assistant Professor, Associate Professor, and Professor.
- **Tenure:** Typically starts at the Associate Professor level.
- **Educational Timeline:**
  - Bachelor's Degree (BS): 4 years
  - Master's Degree (MS): 1-2 years
  - Doctoral Degree (PhD): 4-7 years

## Structure of a Research Paper
- **Title/Author List/Abstract**
- **Background and Motivation**
- **Related Work**
- **Design**
- **Implementation**
- **Evaluation**
- **Analysis**
- **Conclusions**
- **Future Work**

## Self-Publishing and Patents
- **Self-Publishing:** Consider self-publishing your work.
- **Patents:** Understand the costs, time, profit, documentation, and "closed source" nature of patents.

## Parting Thoughts
- **Don't Self-Censor:** Good research can be disruptive. Don't be afraid to tackle controversial topics.
- **Help Others:** Share your knowledge and support your peers.
- **Believe in Yourself:** The research space is less crowded than you might think.
- **Develop a Sense for Open Problems:** Identify and address open problems in your field.
- **Work on Your Own Ideas:** It is often more fulfilling to pursue your own ideas.
- **Keep Pulling the Thread:** Continuously explore and expand your research.
- **Balance Inputs, Processing, and Outputs:** Maintain a balanced approach to your research.
- **Fight Uninformed Law:** Advocate for well-informed and fair laws.
- **Find Inspiration in Others You Respect:** Learn from those you admire.
- **Know What You Don’t Know:** Acknowledge and address unknowns in your research.
- **Don’t Expect to Get Rich:** Pursue research for the love of knowledge, not financial gain.
- **Build Momentum:** Consistently work towards your goals.
- **The Journey Has Many Dividends:** Enjoy the process and the many benefits it brings.
