**Cardoso et al. Springer, 2015.**

## Summary

"Fundamentals of Service Systems" is a comprehensive textbook that delves into the conceptual and practical aspects of the lifecycle of service systems. The book covers all phases, from ideation and design to implementation, analysis, improvement, and trading. Authored by leading experts in the field, this essential resource equips future professionals with the necessary competencies in computer science, engineering, and management, fostering a service-focused mindset.

This textbook serves as a core component for courses on service systems. Each chapter includes a summary, learning objectives, an opening case study, and a review section with questions, a project description, key terms, and a bibliography for further reading. These elements facilitate faster and more effective learning for students.

For researchers, educators, and students interested in the emerging field of service systems, this book provides an overview of the core disciplines involved. It is particularly suitable for students of information systems, information technology, business, and economics. Additionally, it targets business and IT practitioners seeking innovative ways to design, model, analyze, and optimize service systems.

## Distinctive Aspects

- **Interdisciplinary Integration:** Seamlessly integrates the fields of service innovation, design, semantics, analytics, optimization, co-creation, markets, and research.
- **Comprehensive Learning Tools:** Each chapter includes a review section with questions, a project description, a list of key terms, and suggested further reading.
- **Preface by Industry Leader:** Features a preface written by Jim Spohrer, Director of IBM Global University Programs and a key advocate of the Service Science, Management, and Engineering initiative.
- **T-shaped Professional Development:** Aims to train T-shaped professionals who possess both deep expertise in a specific area (e.g., business management or computer science) and a broad set of skills in the field of services (e.g., service design and optimization).
- **Companion Website:** Provides additional materials and activities to support lecturers in their teaching and help students practice and reinforce their learning independently.
