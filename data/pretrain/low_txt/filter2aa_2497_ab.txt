# Advanced Drone Defense Systems

## Introduction
This document provides an overview of various advanced drone defense systems, including net-based defenses, autonomous anti-drone solutions, and electronic countermeasures. It also discusses the legal and practical challenges associated with defending against drones.

## Net-Based Defenses

### Skynet™ Drone Defense
- **Product**: Shotgun Net Shells
- **Price**: ~$20 for 3 shells
- **Demo**: Defeating Net Defenses with Protective Cage
- **Comparison**:
  - Chicken Wire Cage: ~$15
  - SkyNet Drone Net Shotgun Shells (3 pack): ~$20

### DroneCatcher by DelftDynamics
- **Description**: A drone that captures other drones using a net.
- **Price**: ~€30,000
- **Availability**: Expected to ship by the end of 2017
- **References**:
  - [YouTube Demo](http://dronecatcher.nl/)

### Airspace Systems One-Touch Interceptor TI
- **Description**: An autonomous drone that intercepts and eliminates rogue drones.
- **Cost**: Millions of dollars
- **References**:

## Electronic Countermeasures

### DroneShield - DroneGun
- **Description**: An anti-drone gun that jams signals to knock drones out of the sky.
- **Price**: Tens of thousands of dollars
- **Legal Note**: Not allowed for sale or use in the U.S. except by government agencies due to FCC regulations.
- **References**:
  - [DroneShield Website](https://www.droneshield.com/dronegun)

### Battelle - DroneDefender
- **Description**: An electromagnetic gun that shoots down drones.
- **Effectiveness**: Works against Wi-Fi controlled drones but not against cellular/GPS controlled drones.
- **References**:
  - [Battelle Website](https://www.battelle.org/government-offerings/national-security/aerospace-systems/counter-uas-technologies/dronedefender)

### Blighter - AUDS (Anti-UAV Defence System)
- **Description**: Detects, tracks, and disrupts drones using radar and jamming technology.
- **Price**: ~£800k (~ $1,037,656.00 USD)
- **References**:
  - [Blighter Website](http://www.blighter.com/products/auds-anti-uav-defence-system.html)

### DroneDefense - Dynopis E1000MP
- **Description**: A man-portable drone jammer.
- **References**:
  - [DroneDefense Website](http://www.dronedefence.co.uk/dynopis-ECM)

## Hacking and Remote Control

### Leonardo-Finmeccanica - Selex ES - Falcon Shield
- **Description**: An anti-drone system that can take over and control drones.
- **Price**: ~$311k
- **References**:
  - [Leonardo-Finmeccanica Website](http://www.leonardocompany.com/en/-/falcon-shield-launch)

### SkyJack
- **Description**: A Raspberry Pi drone that autonomously seeks out, hacks, and takes remote control of other drones.
- **References**:
  - [Samy Kamkar's Website](http://samy.pl/skyjack/)

### Maldrone
- **Description**: The first malware for drones, allowing remote hijacking of Parrot AR Drones.
- **References**:
  - [Garage4Hackers Article](http://garage4hackers.com/entry.php?b=3105)

- **References**:
  - [Wikipedia Entry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_Weapon_System)

### Counter Directed Energy Weapons
- **Methods**:
  - Mirrors (Dielectric or Bragg)
  - Smoke, Dust, Obscurants
  - Thermal Transport Delay
  - Ablative Materials
  - Metamaterials
  - Adsys Controls – Helio (detects and fights back against lasers)
- **References**:
  - [Popular Science Article](http://www.popsci.com/laser-guns-are-targeting-uavs-but-drones-are-fighting-back)
  - [Office of Naval Research Fact Sheet](https://www.onr.navy.mil/en/Media-Center/Fact-Sheets/Counter-Directed-Energy-Weapons.aspx)

## Legal and Practical Challenges

### FAA Rule on Small Drones
- **Effective Date**: 29 Aug 2016
- **Registration**: [FAA Registration Site](https://registermyuas.faa.gov/)

### Taylor vs. FAA - Lawsuit Against Drone Rule
- **Update**: Oral argument scheduled for March 14, 2017, in the U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit.
- **References**:
  - [RC Groups Update](https://www.rcgroups.com/forums/showthread.php?2561936-Taylor-v-FAA-Update)
  - [DRONELIFE Article](https://dronelife.com/2016/06/14/the-lawsuit-over-drone-registration-taylor-vs-huerta-faa/)

### Legal Implications of Shooting Drones
- **Warning**: Shooting at drones is illegal and can result in severe penalties, including up to 5 years in prison.
- **References**:
  - [Digital Trends Article](https://www.digitaltrends.com/cool-tech/drone-shooting-is-a-federal-crime/)
