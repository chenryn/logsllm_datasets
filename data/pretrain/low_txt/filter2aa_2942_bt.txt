### Appendix J: Resources

#### Relationship to Other Programs and Publications

This revision to NIST SP 800-161 builds upon concepts described in various NIST and other publications to facilitate integration with agencies' existing enterprise-wide activities and to align with legislative developments following its initial release. These resources are complementary and help enterprises build risk-based information security programs to protect their operations and assets against a range of diverse and increasingly sophisticated threats. This publication will be revised to remain consistent with the NIST SP 800-53 security controls catalog using an iterative process as the C-SCRM discipline continues to mature.

##### NIST Publications

This document leverages the latest versions of the publications and programs that guided its initial development, as well as new publications following its initial release:

- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) Version 1.1**
- **FIPS 199, Standards for Security Categorization of Federal Information and Information Systems**: Conducts criticality analysis and scoping C-SCRM activities to high-impact components or systems.
- **NIST SP 800-30, Rev. 1, Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments**: Integrates ICT/OT SCRM into the risk assessment process.
- **NIST SP 800-37, Rev. 2, Risk Management Framework for Information Systems and Organizations**: Provides a system life cycle approach for security and privacy.
- **NIST SP 800-39, Managing Information Security Risk**: Integrates ICT/OT SCRM into the risk management levels and risk management process.
- **NIST SP 800-53, Rev. 5, Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations**: Provides information security controls for enhancing and tailoring to the C-SCRM context.
- **NIST SP 800-53B, Control Baselines for Information Systems and Organizations**: Codifies control baselines and C-SCRM supplementary guidance.
- **NIST SP 800-150, Guide to Cyber Threat Information Sharing**: Provides guidelines for establishing and participating in cyber threat information relationships.
- **NIST SP 800-160 Vol. 1, Systems Security Engineering** and **NIST SP 800-160 Vol. 2, Rev. 1, Developing Cyber Resilient Systems**: Offers specific guidance on the security engineering aspects of C-SCRM.
- **NIST SP 800-171, Rev. 2, Protecting Controlled Information in Nonfederal Systems and Organizations**: Recommends security requirements to protect the confidentiality of CUI.
- **NIST SP 800-172, Enhanced Security Requirements for Protecting Controlled Unclassified Information – A Supplement to NIST Special Publication 800-171**: Recommends enhanced security requirements for protecting the confidentiality of CUI.
- **NIST SP 800-181, Rev. 1, National Initiative for Cybersecurity Education (NICE) Cybersecurity Workforce Framework**: Forms a common lexicon for C-SCRM workforce topics.
- **NISTIR 7622, Notional Supply Chain Risk Management Practices for Federal Information Systems**: Provides background materials for applying the special publication to specific acquisition processes.
- **NISTIR 8179, Criticality Analysis Process Model: Prioritizing Systems and Components**: Guides ratings of supplier criticality.
- **NISTIR 8276, Key Practices in Cyber Supply Chain Risk Management: Observations from Industry**: Elucidates recent C-SCRM trends in the private sector.
- **NISTIR 8286, Identifying and Estimating Cybersecurity Risk for Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)**: Informs the content on integrating C-SCRM into enterprise risk management.
