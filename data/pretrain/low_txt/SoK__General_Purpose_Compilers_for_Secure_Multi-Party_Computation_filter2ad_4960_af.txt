### Benchmarks and Expressive Capabilities

Benchmarks are essential for demonstrating the expressive capabilities of a framework and serve as a baseline for performance comparison. However, standardized benchmarking has several known issues:
- Certain metrics may not be relevant to every protocol.
- Compilers may optimize for performance on specific benchmark problems rather than in general cases.
- The performance measurement issues discussed in Section V-E remain.

We hope that careful design of benchmarking problems will mitigate these issues and provide a useful tool for practitioners in the future.

### Standardization and Benchmarks

Many frameworks are designed around specific features, such as type systems or optimization techniques. Standardizing essential features across frameworks allows researchers to focus on the core aspects of their systems and provides a level of consistency for users. Standardization can also set a more consistent baseline for performance.
