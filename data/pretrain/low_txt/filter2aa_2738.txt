## Dawkins Meme Theory
- **Origin:** Richard Dawkins coined the term "meme" in 1976.
- **Definition:** A unit of cultural information that propagates from one mind to another as a theoretical unit of cultural evolution.
- **Example:** "I want a tattoo"

## Ideosphere
- **Definition:** The sum of all memes in circulation.
- **Concept:** The "Global Human Consciousness"
- **Internet Subset:** A subset of the ideosphere.

## Meme Seepage Theory
- **Assumptions Recap:**
  - **S-Curve Propagation**
  - **Variable Amplitudes and Latencies**
- **Proposition:**
  - **Primary Site Traffic Increase:** Proportional increase in traffic to linked sites.
  - **Reference Sites:** Proportional increase in traffic to sites like Google and top 10 search results.

## Primary Meme Seepage Experiment
- **Experiment:**
  - **Link Posting:** Direct link to IAFF.com to tap off a slice of its traffic via meme seepage.
  - **Expected Result:** Doubling of IAFF traffic should produce a proportional increase in traffic to RealMeme.com.
  - **Actual Result:** No concurrent increase in traffic to RealMeme.com.

## Secondary Seepage Failure
- **Experiment:**
  - **Binding:** Posted an IAFF.com analysis indexed by Google.
  - **Ranking:** Achieved #7 Google result for "IAmFacingForeclosure.com".
  - **Traffic Induction:** Expected traffic from both IAFF.com and Google search results.
  - **Anomalies:** Major discrepancies during a major television event.

## Botnet Proof
- **Indicators:**
  - **Multiple OS per IP**
  - **Dense and Abrupt Traffic Changes**
  - **High 2-Page Hits (Typically 90% 1-Page)**
  - **Non-Human Click Flow**
  - **Bookmarks Only, No Blog Entry Points**
  - **Most Bookmarks Are Older**
- **Logs:**
  - **Same IP**
  - **Density**
  - **Pages**
  - **Entry Points**
