**SAVE THE DATE**

**23rd USENIX Security Symposium**
- **Date:** August 20–22, 2014
- **Location:** San Diego, CA

---

The USENIX Security Symposium is a premier event that brings together researchers, practitioners, system administrators, system programmers, and other professionals interested in the latest advancements in computer system and network security. The symposium will feature a three-day technical program, including:

- **Refereed Papers:** Presenting cutting-edge research and findings.
- **Invited Talks:** Insights from leading experts in the field.
- **Posters:** Visual presentations of ongoing research.
- **Panel Discussions:** Interactive sessions on critical topics.
- **Birds-of-a-Feather (BoF) Sessions:** Informal gatherings for in-depth discussions.

**Featured Speakers and Sessions:**

- **Keynote Address:**
  - **Speaker:** Phil Lapsley, author of *Exploding the Phone: The Untold Story of the Teenagers and Outlaws Who Hacked Ma Bell*

- **Panel: "The Future of Crypto: Getting from Here to Guarantees"**
  - **Panelists:** Daniel J. Bernstein, Matt Blaze, and Tanja Lange

- **Invited Talk: "Battling Human Trafficking with Big Data"**
  - **Speaker:** Rolando R. Lopez, Orphan Secure

- **Invited Talk: "Insight into the NSA’s Weakening of Crypto Standards"**
  - **Speaker:** Joseph Menn, Reuters
