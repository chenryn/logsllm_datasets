**Title: Back to the Future, Part 4: The Internet**

**Author: Simon S. Lam**

**SIGCOMM Award Winner: Simon S. Lam, University of Texas at Austin**

**Keynote Talk: Back to the Future, Part 4: The Internet**

**Award Recognition:**
Simon S. Lam is honored with this award for his visionary, broad, and rigorous contributions to several key areas, including secure network communication, the analysis of network and multiaccess protocols, the analysis of queueing networks, and the design of mechanisms for quality of service.

**Biography:**
Simon S. Lam is a Professor and Regents Chair in Computer Sciences at the University of Texas at Austin. He earned his BSEE degree with Distinction from Washington State University, Pullman, in 1969, and his M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in engineering from UCLA in 1970 and 1974, respectively.

From 1971 to 1974, he served as a Postgraduate Research Engineer at the ARPA Network Measurement Center at UCLA, where he focused on satellite and radio packet switching networks. From 1974 to 1977, he was a Research Staff Member at the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, New York. Since 1977, he has been a faculty member at the University of Texas at Austin, serving as Department Chair from 1992 to 1994. His current research interests include network protocol design and analysis, distributed multimedia, and Internet security services.

Simon has been an active member of the editorial boards of several prestigious journals, including IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking, IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Communications, Proceedings of the IEEE, and Performance Evaluation. He also served as the Editor-in-Chief of IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking from 1995 to 1999 and currently sits on the editorial board of Computer Networks. In 1983, he organized the inaugural ACM SIGCOMM Symposium at the University of Texas at Austin and is a founding Steering Committee member of the IEEE International Conference on Network Protocols.

**Awards and Honors:**
- 2004 W. Wallace McDowell Award from the IEEE Computer Society, with the citation: "For outstanding fundamental contributions in network protocols and security services."
- Co-recipient of the 1975 Leonard G. Abraham Prize from the IEEE Communications Society.
- Co-recipient of the 2001 William R. Bennett Prize from the IEEE Communications Society.
- IEEE Fellow (elected 1985)
- ACM Fellow (elected 1998)

This recognition underscores Simon S. Lam's significant and enduring impact on the field of computer networking and security.
