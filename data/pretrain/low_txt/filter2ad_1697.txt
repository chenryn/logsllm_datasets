### Save the Date
**23rd USENIX Security Symposium**  
**August 20–22, 2014 • San Diego, CA**

The USENIX Security Symposium is a premier event that brings together researchers, practitioners, system administrators, system programmers, and other professionals interested in the latest advancements in computer system and network security. The symposium will feature a comprehensive three-day technical program, including refereed papers, invited talks, poster sessions, panel discussions, and Birds-of-a-Feather (BoF) sessions.

#### Featured Speakers and Sessions:
- **Keynote Address:**  
  *Phil Lapsley, author of "Exploding the Phone: The Untold Story of the Teenagers and Outlaws Who Hacked Ma Bell"*

- **Panel Discussion:**  
  *“The Future of Crypto: Getting from Here to Guarantees”*  
  *Panelists: Daniel J. Bernstein, Matt Blaze, and Tanja Lange*

- **Invited Talk:**  
  *“Battling Human Trafficking with Big Data”*  
  *Rolando R. Lopez, Orphan Secure*

- **Invited Talk:**  
  *“Insight into the NSA’s Weakening of Crypto Standards”*  
  *Joseph Menn, Reuters*
