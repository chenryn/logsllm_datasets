### Future Directions
In the future, we aim to explore secure training on ImageNet-scale datasets. Although our current system can perform inference on commodity machines, secure training will require protocols that can leverage specialized compute and networking hardware. 

Like all previous work on 2PC for secure DNN inference, CrypTFlow2 currently only considers semi-honest adversaries. We plan to extend this to handle malicious adversaries. Another area of interest is to enhance the server's ability to hide the classifier \( F \) from the client during the computation of \( F(x, w) \). Similar to [43], SCIHE can already hide certain aspects of \( F \), such as filter sizes, strides, and whether a layer is convolutional or fully connected. This means SCIHE hides more information than OT-based tools [48] but reveals more than FHE-based tools [13, 31]. We are investigating methods to further conceal information about \( F \) while minimizing overhead.
