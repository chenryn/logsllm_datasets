### Nomenclature

- **\( \text{Avg}_{j \in T} f(j) \)**: The average of \( f(j) \) over \( j \) in the set \( T \). (Page 47)
- **\( M \)**: A (randomized) mechanism \( M : X^n \times Q \rightarrow Y \) or \( M : X^n \rightarrow Y \). (Page 4)
- **\( Q \)**: A \( k \times |X| \) matrix with \(\{0, 1\}\) entries whose rows correspond to a set of counting queries over \( X \). Abusing notation, we also denote this set of counting queries by \( Q \). (Page 37)
- **\( Q \)**: A set of queries \( q : X^n \rightarrow Y \). (Page 4)
- **\( Q_S \)**: The restriction of counting query family (i.e., \(\{0, 1\}\) matrix) \( Q \) to the data universe elements (i.e., columns) in \( S \). (Page 37)
- **\( X \)**: A data universe for dataset rows. (Page 4)
- **\( Y \)**: A (discrete) output space for a mechanism. (Page 4)
- **\( \delta \)**: The additive privacy parameter of differential privacy. (Page 6)
- **\( \text{Disc}(Q) \)**: The discrepancy of matrix \( Q \), i.e., \(\min_{z \in \{\pm 1\}^n} \|Qz\|_\infty\). (Page 35)
- **\( \ell^*(K) \)**: The Gaussian mean width of \( K \): \(\mathbb{E}_g \max_{z \in K} | \langle z, g \rangle |\). (Page 63)
- **\( \epsilon \)**: The multiplicative privacy parameter of differential privacy. (Page 6)
- **\( \text{GS}_q \)**: The global sensitivity of \( q \), i.e., \(\max_{x \sim x'} |q(x) - q(x')|\). (Page 7)
- **\( \text{HerDisc}(Q) \)**: The hereditary discrepancy of matrix \( Q \), i.e., \(\max_{S \subseteq X} \text{Disc}(Q_S)\). (Page 38)
- **\( \text{HerPDisc}(Q) \)**: The hereditary partial discrepancy of matrix \( Q \), i.e., \(\max_{S \subseteq X} \text{PDisc}(Q_S)\). (Page 38)
- **\( \text{Lap}(\sigma) \)**: The Laplace distribution with scale \( \sigma \). (Page 7)
- **\( \ln \)**: The natural logarithm function. (Page 7)
- **\( \log \)**: Base 2 logarithm function. (Page 7)
- **\( \text{LS}_q(x) \)**: The local sensitivity of query \( q \) on dataset \( x \), i.e., \(\max_{x' \sim x} |q(x) - q(x')|\). (Page 21)
- **\( \|v\|_p \)**: The \(\ell_p\) norm of vector \( v \), i.e., \((\sum_i |v_i|^p)^{1/p}\). (Page 35)
- **\( Q^{\text{conj}} = Q^{\text{conj}}(d) \)**: \(\bigcup_{t=0}^d Q^{\text{conj}}_t(d)\), where \( Q^{\text{conj}}_t(d) \) is the set of \( t \)-way marginal queries, i.e., counting queries corresponding to \( t \)-way conjunctions on \( X = \{0, 1\}^d \). (Page 5)
- **\( Q^{\text{conj}}_t \)**: The set of \( t \)-way marginal queries, i.e., counting queries corresponding to \( t \)-way conjunctions on \( X = \{0, 1\}^d \). (Page 5)
- **\( Q^{\text{means}} = Q^{\text{means}}(d) \)**: The set of \( d \) attribute means on a dataset with \( d \) boolean attributes, i.e., counting queries corresponding to coordinate functions on \( X = \{0, 1\}^d \). (Page 5)
- **\( Q^{\text{pt}} = Q^{\text{pt}}(X) \)**: The set of counting queries corresponding to point functions on \( X \). (Page 5)
- **\( Q^{\text{thr}} = Q^{\text{thr}}(X) \)**: The set of counting queries corresponding to threshold functions on totally ordered \( X \). (Page 5)
- **\( \text{SD}(Y, Y') \)**: The statistical distance between random variables \( Y \) and \( Y' \). (Page 9)
- **\( \sigma_{\text{min}}(M) \)**: The smallest singular value of matrix \( M \), i.e., \(\inf_{z \neq 0} \frac{\|Mz\|_2}{\|z\|_2}\). (Page 39)
- **\( \text{Supp}(Z) \)**: The support of random variable \( Z \), i.e., \(\{z : \Pr[Z = z] > 0\}\). (Page 10)
- **\( \text{VC}(Q) \)**: The Vapnik-Chervonenkis dimension of \( Q \), i.e., the largest number \( k \) such that there exist \( x_1, \ldots, x_k \in X \) for which \(\{(q(x_1), \ldots, q(x_k)) : q \in Q\} = \{0, 1\}^k\). (Page 27)
- **\( D(p \| q) \)**: The Kullback-Leibler divergence (a.k.a. relative entropy) between discrete probability measures \( p \) and \( q \), i.e., \(\sum_y p(y) \cdot \log \left( \frac{p(y)}{q(y)} \right)\). (Page 16)
- **\( d(x, x') \)**: The Hamming distance between datasets \( x, x' \in X^n \). (Page 8)
- **\( K \)**: The convex hull of the answer vectors \( a_w = (q(w))_{q \in Q} \in \mathbb{R}^Q \) over \( w \in X \). (Page 44)
- **\( n \)**: The number of rows in a dataset. (Page 4)
- **\( P_\alpha(K) \)**: The largest number of points that can be packed in \( K \) with all pairwise \(\ell_\infty\) distances larger than \( \alpha \). (Page 44)
- **\( q : X \rightarrow \{0, 1\} \)**: A predicate inducing a counting query \( q : X^n \rightarrow [0, 1] \). (Page 5)
- **\( q : X^n \rightarrow Y \)**: A query. (Page 5)
- **\( x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n) \in X^n \)**: A dataset of \( n \) rows. (Page 4)
- **\( x \sim x' \)**: Datasets \( x, x' \in X^n \) differ in one row. (Page 6)
