### SAVE THE DATE

**23rd USENIX Security Symposium**
- **Dates:** August 20–22, 2014
- **Location:** San Diego, CA

---

The USENIX Security Symposium is a premier event that convenes researchers, practitioners, system administrators, system programmers, and other professionals interested in the latest advancements in the security of computer systems and networks. The symposium will feature a comprehensive 3-day technical program, including:

- **Refereed Papers:** Presenting cutting-edge research and findings.
- **Invited Talks:** Keynote addresses and presentations by leading experts.
- **Posters:** Visual displays of ongoing research and projects.
- **Panel Discussions:** Interactive sessions on critical security topics.
- **Birds-of-a-Feather (BoF) Sessions:** Informal gatherings for in-depth discussions.

#### Featured Speakers and Sessions:

- **Keynote Address:**
  - **Speaker:** Phil Lapsley, author of *Exploding the Phone: The Untold Story of the Teenagers and Outlaws Who Hacked Ma Bell*

- **Panel Discussion:**
  - **Topic:** "The Future of Crypto: Getting from Here to Guarantees"
  - **Panelists:**
    - Daniel J. Bernstein
    - Matt Blaze
    - Tanja Lange

- **Invited Talk:**
  - **Title:** "Battling Human Trafficking with Big Data"
  - **Speaker:** Rolando R. Lopez, Orphan Secure

- **Invited Talk:**
  - **Title:** "Insight into the NSA’s Weakening of Crypto Standards"
  - **Speaker:** Joseph Menn, Reuters
