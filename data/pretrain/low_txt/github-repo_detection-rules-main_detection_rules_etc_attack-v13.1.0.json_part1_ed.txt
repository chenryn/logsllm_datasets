APT28 has also used a machine to relay and obscure communications between CHOPSTICK and their server.

**Detection of ACL and File/Directory Ownership Modifications**
- **Description**: Many commands used to modify ACLs and file/directory ownership are built-in system utilities and may generate a high false positive alert rate. Compare against baseline knowledge for how systems are typically used and correlate modification events with other indications of malicious activity where possible. Commonly abused command arguments include `chmod +x`, `chmod -R 755`, and `chmod 777`.
