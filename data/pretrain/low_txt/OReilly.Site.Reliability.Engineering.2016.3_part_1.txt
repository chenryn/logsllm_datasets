# Site Reliability Engineering: How Google Runs Production Systems
**Edited by Betsy Beyer, Chris Jones, Jennifer Petoff, and Niall Richard Murphy**

## Praise for Site Reliability Engineering

Google’s SREs have provided an invaluable service to the industry by documenting the principles, practices, and patterns—both architectural and cultural—that enable their teams to achieve continuous delivery and world-class reliability at an unprecedented scale. This book is a must-read for anyone seeking to enhance their organization's operational efficiency and reliability.

—Jez Humble, coauthor of *Continuous Delivery* and *Lean Enterprise*

When Google first began presenting at systems administration conferences, it was akin to hearing a Gila monster expert speak at a reptile show—fascinating, but not immediately applicable. However, the operational landscape has since evolved, and the best practices honed by Google's SREs are now highly relevant to organizations of all sizes. For those grappling with scalability, reliability, and operational challenges, this book is a timely and essential resource.

—David N. Blank-Edelman, Director, USENIX Board of Directors, and founding co-organizer of SREcon

I've been eagerly awaiting this book ever since I left Google. It encapsulates the gospel I preach to my colleagues in production engineering.

—Björn Rabenstein, Team Lead of Production Engineering at SoundCloud, Prometheus developer, and former Google SRE

This comprehensive guide to Site Reliability Engineering, written by the company that pioneered the concept, delves into both the technical details and the underlying thought processes, goals, and lessons learned. If you want to understand what SRE truly means, start here.

—Russ Allbery, SRE and Security Engineer

Google employees have generously shared the processes, including the missteps, that have enabled Google services to scale and maintain high reliability. Anyone aiming to build scalable, integrated services should read this book, as it provides an insider’s perspective on creating and maintaining robust systems.

—Rik Farrow, USENIX

Developing large-scale services like Gmail is challenging, and running them with high reliability, especially while making daily changes, is even more so. This comprehensive guide offers insights into Google's approach, allowing readers to learn from their experiences and avoid common pitfalls.

—Urs Hölzle, SVP Technical Infrastructure, Google
