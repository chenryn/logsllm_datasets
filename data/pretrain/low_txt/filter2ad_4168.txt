### The Good, the Bad, and the Provable

**Keynote Address by Martín Abadi**

**Bio:**

Martín Abadi is a distinguished Professor of Computer Science at the University of California, Santa Cruz (since 2001) and a Principal Researcher at Microsoft Research, Silicon Valley (since 2006). He completed his studies at Stanford University and has worked at Digital’s System Research Center and other industrial research laboratories. His research primarily focuses on computer and network security, programming languages, and formal verification.

Abadi has made significant contributions to the design and analysis of security protocols and the foundational aspects of object-oriented languages. In 2007, his pioneering work in security was recognized with the Outstanding Innovation Award from the ACM Special Interest Group on Security, Audit, and Control (ACM SIGSAC), as well as the Hall of Fame Award from the ACM Special Interest Group on Operating Systems.
