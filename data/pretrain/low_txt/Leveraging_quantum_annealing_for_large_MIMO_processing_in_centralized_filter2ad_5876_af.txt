### A. QUBO Forms

We demonstrate how to transform 2 × 2 BPSK MIMO Maximum Likelihood (ML) detection into the QUBO form. ML detection solves Eq. 1, where:

\[
\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{H} \mathbf{x} + \mathbf{v}
\]

Here, \(\mathbf{y}\) is the received vector, \(\mathbf{H}\) is the channel matrix, \(\mathbf{x}\) is the transmitted vector, and \(\mathbf{v}\) is the noise vector. For a 2 × 2 BPSK MIMO system, these vectors and matrices can be written as:

\[
\mathbf{y} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
y_{I,1} + j y_{Q,1} \\
y_{I,2} + j y_{Q,2}
\end{pmatrix},
\quad
\mathbf{H} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
h_{I,11} + j h_{Q,11} & h_{I,12} + j h_{Q,12} \\
h_{I,21} + j h_{Q,21} & h_{I,22} + j h_{Q,22}
\end{pmatrix},
\quad
\mathbf{x} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
x_1 \\
x_2
\end{pmatrix},
\quad
\mathbf{v} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
v_{I,1} + j v_{Q,1} \\
v_{I,2} + j v_{Q,2}
\end{pmatrix}
\]

where \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are the BPSK symbols, and \(y_{I,i}\), \(y_{Q,i}\), \(h_{I,ij}\), \(h_{Q,ij}\), \(v_{I,i}\), and \(v_{Q,i}\) are the real and imaginary parts of the respective vectors and matrices.
