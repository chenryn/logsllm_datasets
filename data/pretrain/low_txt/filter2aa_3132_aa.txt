# Sources of Electromagnetic Radiation (EMR)

## Common Sources of EMR
- **Human Brain Waves:**
  - Delta (δ): 0.1 Hz to 3 Hz
  - Theta (θ): 3 Hz to 8 Hz
  - Alpha (α): 8 Hz to 12 Hz
  - Low Beta: 12 Hz to 15 Hz
  - Mid Beta: 15 Hz to 18 Hz
  - High Beta: 18 Hz to 30 Hz
  - Gamma (γ): 30 Hz and above

- **Power Lines:**
  - 50 Hz (Europe, Asia)
  - 60 Hz (North America)

- **Induction Heating:**
  - 100 Hz to 400 Hz (e.g., aircraft power systems)

- **Cell Phones:**
  - 87.7 MHz to 92.1 MHz (FM Radio)
  - 96.1 MHz to 100.1 MHz (FM Radio)
  - 104.1 MHz to 107.9 MHz (FM Radio)

- **Microwave Ovens:**
  - 2.4 GHz

- **Radar:**
  - X-Band: 8 GHz to 12 GHz
  - Ku-Band: 12.5 GHz to 18 GHz
  - K-Band: 18 GHz to 27 GHz
  - Ka-Band: 27.25 GHz to 40 GHz

- **Light Bulbs:**
  - Visible Light: 400 nm to 700 nm

- **Radio Towers:**
  - AM Radio: 540 kHz to 1600 kHz
  - FM Radio: 87.7 MHz to 107.9 MHz

- **X-Ray Machines:**
  - 0.01 nm to 10 nm

- **Radioactive Elements:**
  - Emit various types of radiation, including gamma rays and X-rays
