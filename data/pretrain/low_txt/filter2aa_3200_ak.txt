### Portland UASI: Interoperable Communications System Development

The 5-county Portland Urban Area Security Initiative (UASI) group is developing a multi-frequency Radio Interconnect system, operating at 150 MHz, 450 MHz, and 800 MHz. Additionally, they are implementing a Computer-Aided Dispatch (CAD) to CAD operating system. This integrated system will interlink 911 centers and databases, significantly enhancing interoperability.

### Pennsylvania State Summary

**May 2006**

**State Interoperable Communications: DHS Funded Activities**

- **Total Funding (FY 2003 - FY 2005)**: $25,511,673.39
- **Current Initiatives**:
  - **Tri-State Interoperable Communications System**: Pennsylvania, through the Philadelphia UASI, is leading the development of a state-of-the-art tri-state interoperable communications system impacting Delaware, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania. This system will provide voice and data interoperability across state and municipal boundaries.
  - **Statewide 800 MHz Public Safety Radio Network**: Pennsylvania is completing the rollout of statewide voice and data interoperability, connecting key state agencies, county emergency managers, and 9-1-1 centers.
  - **Satellite-Based Interoperable Voice and Data Network**: Upgrading the network to include state health department locations and Pennsylvania State Police consolidated dispatch centers.
