**Current Interoperable Communications Initiatives:**
- **Statewide Warning and Alerting Systems (EMnet):** Phases III and IV of the EMnet installation are complete, and Phase V is underway. EMnet terminals have been installed in all county warning points, regional hospital command centers, key state agencies, and broadcast radio stations.
- **Incident Scene Management and Interoperability:**
  - Nine regional communications suites (ITECS) have been delivered, with a tenth suite assigned to the Illinois Emergency Management Agency.
  - Mobile interoperable radios have been ordered and distributed to statewide mutual aid organizations.
  - Interoperable communications have been installed in 13 Mobile Command vehicles.
- **Infrastructure to Support Interoperable Scene Communications:**
  - Equipment for the Medical Emergency Radio System of Illinois (MERCI) has been ordered.
  - Installation of STARCOM21 800 MHz radios at public safety agencies throughout the state is in progress.
  - The Illinois Radio Emergency Assistance Channel (IREACH) has been established to improve interoperability between first responders and public health agencies.

**Current Interoperable Communications Initiatives:**
- **Project Hoosier SAFE-T (Safety Acting for Everyone - Together):** This project aims to develop a statewide 800 MHz interoperable, wireless public safety communication system. With 91 of 126 communication sites completed, approximately 80% of Indiana is covered at 95% reliability, including 69 of 92 counties and 20,000 users. The system is scheduled for completion in 2007.
- **Funding Mechanism:** Partial funding was leveraged through an existing small fee attached to vehicle registrations, allowing the project to be completed 3 months early and $12 million under budget.
- **Midwest Public Safety Communication Coalition (MPSCC):** An alliance with Michigan, Illinois, Kentucky, and Ohio, dedicated to creating regional interoperability.

**Current Interoperable Communications Initiatives:**
- **Interoperable Voice, Data, and Video System for Command Operations:** This project in Region 5 (17 counties in southeast Iowa) aims to enable real-time information sharing between EOCs, including voice, data, and video communications. The state is exploring the possibility of expanding this system to other regions.
- **Statewide Communications Plan:** Similar to the Tactical Interoperable Communications Plan (TICP), this plan will include voice, data, and video communications. The governance structure is currently in development.
- **Tri-State Homeland Security First Responder Communications Interoperability Project:** A $6 million grant from DHS/ICE to increase interoperability within the region.
