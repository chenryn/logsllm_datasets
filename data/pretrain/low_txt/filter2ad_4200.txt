**23rd USENIX Security Symposium**
**August 20–22, 2014 • San Diego, CA**

The USENIX Security Symposium is a premier event that brings together researchers, practitioners, system administrators, system programmers, and other professionals interested in the latest advancements in computer systems and network security. The symposium will feature a three-day technical program, including refereed papers, invited talks, poster sessions, panel discussions, and Birds-of-a-Feather (BoF) sessions.

**Featured Sessions and Speakers:**
- **Keynote Address:** Phil Lapsley, author of *Exploding the Phone: The Untold Story of the Teenagers and Outlaws Who Hacked Ma Bell*
- **Panel Discussion:** "The Future of Crypto: Getting from Here to Guarantees" with Daniel J. Bernstein, Matt Blaze, and Tanja Lange
- **Invited Talk:** "Battling Human Trafficking with Big Data" by Rolando R. Lopez, Orphan Secure
- **Invited Talk:** "Insight into the NSA’s Weakening of Crypto Standards" by Joseph Menn, Reuters

**Co-Located Events (August 18–19, 2014):**

- **CSET '14:** 7th Workshop on Cyber Security Experimentation and Test

- **HealthTech '14:** 2014 USENIX Summit on Health Information Technologies
