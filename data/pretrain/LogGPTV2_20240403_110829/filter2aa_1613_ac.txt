### Overview of the Normal Distribution
- The normal distribution is characterized by data points that fall within three standard deviations of the mean.
- This brief overview of the normal distribution should help in understanding the subsequent analysis.

### Importance of Normality
- **Parametric vs. Non-Parametric Analysis**:
  - Parametric analysis (e.g., Pearson's correlation) requires continuous data that approximates a normal distribution and a linear relationship between variables.
  - Non-parametric analysis (e.g., Spearman's correlation) can be used on rank order data or continuous data that does not meet the assumptions of parametric tests.

- **Pearson's Correlation**:
  - More powerful and preferable for continuous, normally distributed data.
  - Examines the magnitude of differences between data points.

- **Spearman's Correlation**:
  - Applicable to rank order data or non-normally distributed continuous data.
  - Converts nominal values into ranks, potentially losing the magnitude of differences.

- **Central Limit Theorem**:
  - States that with sufficiently large sample sizes, the sample means will approximate a normal distribution.
  - Despite this, the decision was made to use Spearman's test due to the following reasons:
    1. Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests indicated non-normality.
    2. Uncertainty about the underlying population distributions.
    3. Large sample size makes Spearman's only slightly less powerful than Pearson's.

### Statistical Significance and Real-World Implications
- **P-Value**:
  - Indicates the probability of observing the results if the null hypothesis (no relationship) is true.
  - A smaller p-value provides stronger evidence against the null hypothesis.

- **Correlation Coefficient (r)**:
  - Measures the strength and direction of the relationship.
  - Values close to 1 or -1 indicate a strong relationship.

- **Coefficient of Determination (r²)**:
  - Represents the percentage of variance in one variable explained by the other.
  - For example, an r value of 0.24 (r² = 0.05) indicates that only 5% of the variance is explained.
