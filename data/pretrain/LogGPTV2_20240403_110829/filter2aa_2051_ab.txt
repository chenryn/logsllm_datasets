Software Translation
• Natural Language Processing (NLP): the subfield of 
artificial intelligence and linguistics that studies the 
processing of NL (English, Dutch, Russian, etc)
– Devoted to making computers "understand" human languages
• Machine translation (MT): computer translation of texts 
from one natural language to another 
– Considers grammatical structure 
– Renders up to 80% accuracy
– Draft-quality, not for literature or legal texts
– Humans still need to pre- and post-edit (proof-read)
– Ultimate goal is no human intervention

`smash the stack` [C programming] n. On many C 
implementations it is possible to corrupt the execution stack by
writing past the end of an array declared auto in a routine.  Code 
that does this is said to smash the stack, and can cause return 
from the routine to jump to a random address.  This can produce 
some of the most insidious data-dependent bugs known to 
mankind. Variants include trash the stack, scribble the stack, 
mangle the stack; the term mung the stack is not used, as this is 
never done intentionally. See spam; see also alias bug, fandango
on core, memory leak, precedence lossage, overrun screw.
