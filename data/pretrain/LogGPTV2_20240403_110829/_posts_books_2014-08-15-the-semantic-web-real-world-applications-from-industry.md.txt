**Authors:** Cardoso, Hepp, Lytras  
**Publisher:** Springer, 2008

### Overview

"The Semantic Web: Real-World Applications from Industry" offers a comprehensive look at the practical applications of semantic technologies in real-world business environments. For years, academic research and early industrial prototypes have shown that semantic technology can significantly enhance human and machine access to the vast and rapidly growing amount of information available on the World Wide Web. Now, these technologies are transitioning from research into real-world products and applications.

### Content

This book presents a series of case studies that illustrate how businesses can derive tangible benefits from the adoption of semantic technology in various sectors, such as telecommunications, B2B integration, healthcare, education, and more. Each case study provides a detailed description of the challenges faced, the technical solutions implemented, and the actual results achieved. Additionally, the book includes valuable lessons learned, which will be instrumental for future investments in semantic technology.

### Contributions

Written by leading experts in the field of semantic technology from diverse backgrounds and countries, including industry, academia, and government agencies, this volume combines state-of-the-art expertise with practical experiences. The contributors provide a balanced mix of theoretical insights and practical advice, making it a valuable resource for both professionals and researchers.

### Audience

"The Semantic Web: Real-World Applications from Industry" is designed for industry professionals and researchers interested in the current state and future potential of semantic technology. It is also suitable as an advanced-level reference text for students in computer science.

### Foreword

The book includes a foreword by Michael L. Brodie, Chief Scientist at Verizon Communications, Cambridge, USA.
