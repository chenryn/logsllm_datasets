### Combined Approaches

We also evaluated existing solutions that use a combined approach and summarized which standalone techniques these solutions incorporated. These works are primarily focused on defending against SPIT (Spam over Internet Telephony), and some may include SPIT-specific techniques not listed in our table.

#### Weighted Scoring
The weighted scoring approach is used to evaluate and combine multiple techniques based on their effectiveness. We found this approach in related works by Dantu and Kolan [101], Niccolini and Quitek et al. [96], [97], Schlegel et al. [98], Hansen et al. [102], and Mathieu et al. [103].

#### Conditional Procedures
Conditional procedures combine several techniques based on predefined rules or policies. This allows for greater flexibility, such as using different sequences of standalone techniques based on recipient preferences or caller reputation. We found this approach in works by d'Heureuse et al. [104], Dritsas et al. [105], Scata and La Corte [106], and Soupionis and Gritzalis [47].

### Related Work

While this paper compares and analyzes state-of-the-art research in telephone spam defense, it is important to discuss related survey papers. Most of these papers focus on spam in the VoIP domain, specifically SPIT, rather than the larger PSTN telephony network.

Keromytis [107], [108] presented comprehensive surveys of VoIP security, summarizing previous works related to VoIP security and organizing them according to an extended version of the VoIP Security Alliance (VoIPSA) Threat Taxonomy. These surveys reviewed many previous works addressing every type of VoIP threat, including social threats like spamming.

### Conclusion

The various strategies for combining techniques outlined in Section VI will help improve future telephone spam defenses. Our analysis, while requiring some subjective judgment, was conducted to the best of our abilities. We believe that the insights provided will be valuable for future research and development in this field.
