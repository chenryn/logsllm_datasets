### Data Collection and Ethics

Our data collection process involved a group of 10 students, including the first two co-authors of the paper, under the supervision of the fourth co-author. We conducted controlled experimental sessions, each lasting approximately 3-4 hours. During these sessions, volunteering students (the number of participants varied per session) were provided with smartphones by the research team to access one of the apps listed in Appendix B. Beverages and food were offered to the students during the experimental sessions. After each session, the research team retrieved the devices, extracted the logs, and anonymized the data.

To ensure privacy, we used mobile devices purchased specifically for the project rather than the personal devices of the students. Additionally, we did not store any personal information of the students. Consequently, the data collection process did not require institutional review board (IRB) approval.
