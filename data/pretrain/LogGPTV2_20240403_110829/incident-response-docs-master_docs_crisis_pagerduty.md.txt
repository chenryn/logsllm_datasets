## Configuring PagerDuty for Crisis Response Management

Follow these steps to set up your Crisis Response Management instance in PagerDuty:

### 1. **PagerDuty Mobile App**
   - **Action**: Ensure all team members install and configure the [PagerDuty Mobile App](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/mobile-app) to ensure maximum reachability.

### 2. **User Management**
   - **Action**: Add your Executive Crisis Leadership and Crisis Response Team members to the system using the [User Management feature](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/users#add-users).

### 3. **Contact Information**
   - **Action**: Instruct each member to log into the web application and update their profile information, including phone, email, and SMS contact details, especially if they have changed devices. 
   - **Reference**: [Updating User Profile](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/user-profile)
   - **Image**: ![PagerDuty user contact information settings](../assets/img/crisis/09_usercontactinfo.png)

### 4. **Notification Rules**
   - **Action**: Each member should set their high urgency, low urgency, handoff, and subscriber notification rules under their profile.
   - **Reference**: [Setting Notification Rules](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/user-profile#notification-rules)
   - **Image**: ![Use multiple contact methods for high urgency incidents](../assets/img/crisis/10_highurgencynotifications.png)

### 5. **Teams**
   - **Action**: Create teams for your Executive Crisis Leadership, each Crisis Team Leader, and essential support functions such as Crisis Communications, IT, or Legal.
   - **Reference**: [Creating Teams](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/teams)

### 6. **Services**
   - **Action**: Create and configure a service for each crisis category led by your Crisis Team Leaders, such as supply chain, human resources, critical infrastructure, geopolitics, and physical security.
   - **Reference**: [Creating a Service](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/services-and-integrations#create-a-service)

### 7. **Urgency Settings**
   - **Action**: Set the notification urgency for each service (high, low, dynamic, or based on operating hours).
   - **Reference**: [Service Settings](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/service-settings#notification-urgency)

### 8. **Escalation Policies**
   - **Action**: Define who gets notified first and how long before the notification escalates to the next team member. Configure round-robin scheduling if you wish to alternate per crisis.
   - **Reference**: [Creating Escalation Policies](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/escalation-policies#create-an-escalation-policy)
   - **Image**: ![Escalation policies determine which responders are contacted](../assets/img/crisis/11_escalationpolicy.png)

### 9. **Integrations**
   - **Action**: Add integrations for instant messaging, video conferencing tools, or create custom email integrations or connections to other systems for triggering alerts.
   - **Reference**: [Adding Integrations](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/services-and-integrations#add-integrations-to-an-existing-service)

### 10. **Schedules**
   - **Action**: Create on-call rotations for the teams associated with each crisis service.
   - **Reference**: [Creating Schedules](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/schedule-basics#create-a-schedule)
   - **Image**: ![Using multiple layers in schedules helps teams create full coverage](../assets/img/crisis/12_schedulelayers.png)

### 11. **Incident Priority**
   - **Action**: Add your custom classification scheme for crisis response escalation levels.
   - **Reference**: [Incident Priority](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/incident-priority)

### 12. **Incident Workflows**
   - **Action**: Create workflows for each crisis based on conditions such as priority, status, and urgency using system templates or from scratch.
   - **Reference**: [Creating Incident Workflows](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/incident-workflows)
   - **Image**: ![Incident workflows can help with communication and coordination](../assets/img/crisis/13_incidentworkflows.png)

### 13. **On-Call Readiness Report**
   - **Action**: Confirm that your teams are on-call ready and properly configured.
   - **Reference**: [On-Call Readiness Reports](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/on-call-readiness-reports)

### 14. **Postmortem Template**
   - **Action**: Configure your postmortem template to fit your needs after a crisis.
   - **Reference**: [Customizing Postmortem Templates](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/postmortems#customize-the-postmortem-template)

### 15. **Status Pages**
   - **Action**: Configure your status page templates for internal stakeholders.
   - **Reference**: [Configuring Status Pages](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/status-pages)
   - **Image**: ![Use status updates to communicate with stakeholders](../assets/img/crisis/14_incidentstatusupdates.png)

By following these steps, you can ensure that your organization is well-prepared to manage and respond to crises effectively using PagerDuty.
