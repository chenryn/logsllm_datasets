Problem Overview: The task involves analyzing logs to determine if they indicate a warning, an error, or a critical fault. The goal is to differentiate between serious issues (output True) and non-error informational messages (output False).
Key Challenges:
Identifying the context of log messages, distinguishing between severity levels, and applying consistent criteria for classification across varied log formats.
Solution Steps:
1. Identify keywords or phrases indicating error or warning severity (e.g., 'error', 'critical', 'failed').
2. Assess the presence of contextually important phrases that suggest non-critical information (e.g., 'informational', 'no enough resource').
3. Classify the log message based on identified keywords: if any severity keywords are found, mark as True; otherwise, mark as False.
4. Consider additional contextual clues such as the structure of log messages and common phrases in similar logs.
5. Output the final classification based on the analysis.
