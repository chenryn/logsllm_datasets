Problem Overview: The objective is to classify log entries as either indicating a warning, error, or serious fault (output True), or as informational (output False). This involves analyzing the content of log messages to determine their severity.
Key Challenges:
Distinguishing between critical error messages and non-critical informational messages can be ambiguous. The challenge lies in identifying specific keywords or patterns that signal warnings or errors amidst a variety of log formats.
Solution Steps:
1. Identify keywords and phrases commonly associated with warnings, errors, and critical faults in logs.
2. Parse the log entry to extract the relevant components and check for the presence of identified keywords.
3. Determine the context of the log message based on its structure and the presence of severity indicators.
4. If any keywords indicating a warning or error are found, classify the log as True; otherwise, classify as False.
5. Validate the classification against known log patterns to ensure accuracy.
